How It Works

No towers. No apps.
Just radio.

01 // BROADCAST

Your location, over the air

The Wayfinder locks onto GPS satellites and broadcasts your coordinates over LoRa radio. No pairing, no setup — power it on and you're on the air.

02 // RECEIVE

See who's nearby

When another Wayfinder is in range, your badge receives their ping — name, status message, GPS position, and a color you assign to them on your display.

03 // NAVIGATE

The ring points the way

A ring of LEDs acts as a live compass, rotating to point directly toward other badge holders. No map. No screen to stare at. Just look at the light.

04 // STATUS

Broadcast a message

Pre-save status messages and broadcast them alongside your coordinates. "Meet here." "Point of interest." Anything you want — 23 characters, direct to every badge in range.

Hardware
  • ESP32-S3 microcontroller
  • Custom 4-layer mainboard PCB
  • LoRa radio — long-range, low-power
  • GPS module with TinyGPS+ parsing
  • Magnetometer / IMU for compass heading
  • OLED display (128×32)
  • LED ring — compass and direction indicator
  • LED strip — built-in flashlight
  • Rotary encoder + 4 buttons for input
Power
  • Single 18650 Li-ion cell
  • TP4056 charging IC — USB charging
  • Solid-state soft power switch
  • Auto-shutdown at low battery to protect the cell
  • DC-DC boost converter for 5V LED rail
Firmware
  • FreeRTOS — fully task-based architecture
  • Flash-persisted saved message list
  • RPC interface for external configuration
  • Open source — MIT licensed
